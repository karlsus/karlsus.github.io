How to get to Palawan


By air

From/To Manila
Some airlines which are available everyday are Air Asia, Cebu Pacific and Philippine Airlines. Traveling from Manila to Palawan takes about 1 hour and 15 minutes.


From/To Busuanga/El Nido
Flights from Manila to Puerto Princesa via Busuanga, Cuyo and El Nido, are available two to three times a week via South East Asian Airlines. SKYJET Airlines also offer flights from Manila to Busuanga. Travel time from Busuanga to Puerto Princesa is only about 1 hour, while flying time from El Nido to Puerto Princes only takes about 30 minutes.


From/To Cebu/Iloilo/Davao
There are also flights from/to Cebu, Iloilo, Davao and Puerto Princesa operated by Cebu Pacific.


Puerto Princesa is accessible mainly from Manila, the premier gateway of the Philippines. For foreigners, here are a few airlines which provide flights to Manila:

By Sea


From/To Cuyo/Iloilo/Cebu
Traveling by sea is available once a week via Montenegro Shipping and Milagrosa Shipping.


From/To Manila
On Thursdays, Negros Navigation sails from Manila to Puerto Princesa. The ship comes back to Manila every Saturday. Superferry also leaves Manila to go to Puerto Princesa every Friday, and comes back to Manila every Sunday. Travel time from Manila to Puerto Princesa, including a stopover at Coron, is between 18 to 22 hours.


From/To Coron
Negros Navigation and Superferry also operates ferries from Coron to Puerto Princesa. For Negros Navigation, it leaves every Friday and comes back to Coron every Saturday. For Superferry, it leaves Coron evey Saturday ang goes back on Sundays.



Around Palawan

In moving around the city, tricycles can be used at a minimal fare. Multicabs and jeepneys also move around the city following specific routes. To travel to the outskirts of town, jeepneys or mini-buses may be used. There are also car rental services available around the area, where you may rent some vans, motorbikes and bicycles. Big groups may book some coasters or bus services too through travel agencies. Shuttle services from Puerto Princesa to El Nido takes about 6 hours, and those from Puerto Princesa to Taytay takes only 4 hours. During wet season, from June to September, some roads are not so accessible. Another way to travel around the area is by sea. There are also motorized outrigger boats you can use to transport to islands and coastal beaches.
